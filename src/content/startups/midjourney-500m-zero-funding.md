# Midjourney: $500M Revenue, Zero Funding, 40 Employees

Midjourney generates approximately $500 million in annual revenue with 40 employees and zero external funding. That is $12.5 million in revenue per employee — a ratio that makes even the most efficient tech companies look bloated. It is arguably the most impressive business in the entire AI industry.

## The Numbers That Matter

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Annual revenue | ~$500M |
| Employees | ~40 |
| Revenue per employee | $12.5M |
| External funding raised | $0 |
| Subscribers | 16M+ |
| Average revenue per user | ~$31/month |
| Estimated profit margin | 70-80% |
| Estimated annual profit | $350-400M |

For comparison: OpenAI has 3,000+ employees generating $13B (revenue per employee: $4.3M). Anthropic has 1,200+ employees generating $5B ($4.2M per employee). Midjourney generates 3x more revenue per person than the two AI giants combined.

## How They Make Money

Midjourney operates exclusively through Discord with a simple subscription model:

- **Basic Plan:** $10/month — 200 image generations
- **Standard Plan:** $30/month — 15 hours of fast generation
- **Pro Plan:** $60/month — 30 hours of fast generation + stealth mode
- **Mega Plan:** $120/month — 60 hours of fast generation

No free tier. No freemium. No advertising. Just subscriptions. This simplicity is part of the magic — there is no sales team, no enterprise contracts, no complicated pricing negotiations.

## Why This Is Extraordinary

**Zero dilution.** Founder David Holz owns the entire company. At a conservative 10x revenue multiple, Midjourney is worth $5 billion+. Holz did not give away a single percentage point to investors. In an era where AI startups raise billions and dilute founders to single-digit ownership, this is remarkable.

**Profitability from day one.** While OpenAI lost $5 billion in 2024 and Anthropic burns through capital at staggering rates, Midjourney has been profitable since launch. The estimated $350-400M annual profit funds all R&D internally.

**No enterprise sales team.** The product sells itself. 16 million subscribers found Midjourney through word of mouth, social media, and the quality of the output. Zero sales reps. Zero marketing budget (effectively).

## What AI Entrepreneurs Can Learn

**1. You do not need to raise money.** The most profitable AI company raised $0. Venture capital is a tool, not a requirement.

**2. Simple pricing wins.** Four tiers, no enterprise negotiation, no custom deals. Every customer pays the same way.

**3. Quality is marketing.** Midjourney's images are so distinctive that every shared image is a billboard. The product IS the marketing.

**4. Stay small.** 40 people generating $500M means every person is essential and highly compensated. No bureaucracy, no middle management, no waste.

**5. Build for consumers first.** While most AI startups chase enterprise contracts, Midjourney proved that millions of individuals will pay $10-120/month for AI tools that make them more creative.

## The Bottom Line

Midjourney is proof that the best AI business is not always the one that raises the most money — it is the one that makes the most money per person, per dollar, per decision. $500M revenue, 40 people, zero funding. This is what an efficiently run AI company looks like.
