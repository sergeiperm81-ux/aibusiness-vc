# White Label AI Chatbot Business: How to Resell AI Chatbots Under Your Brand

The conversational AI market hit $13.2 billion in 2025 and is growing at 21.8% CAGR through 2030 (MarketsandMarkets). Businesses are deploying chatbots faster than ever, but most lack the technical expertise to build and manage them internally. That gap creates one of the most accessible AI business models of 2026: white-label AI chatbot reselling.

The model is straightforward. You purchase chatbot infrastructure from a white-label provider at wholesale pricing, brand it as your own, and sell it to local businesses and agencies at a markup. No coding required. According to a 2025 survey by Chatbot Magazine, 67% of businesses with fewer than 200 employees plan to implement AI chatbots within the next 12 months, yet only 12% have the internal capability to do so. That 55% gap is your addressable market.

Top white-label chatbot resellers report margins of 60-75% on recurring revenue. A reseller with 20 active clients paying $300/mo generates $6,000/mo in revenue with approximately $1,800 in platform costs, netting $4,200/mo in profit. This guide covers the platforms, pricing, target clients, and scaling playbook to build a white-label AI chatbot business from scratch.

## Top White-Label AI Chatbot Platforms

Choosing the right platform determines your capabilities, costs, and client satisfaction. Here are the leading options evaluated for resellers.

### Stammer.ai

Stammer.ai is purpose-built for agencies and resellers. Their white-label plan starts at $497/mo and includes unlimited chatbot deployments, custom branding, and a client management dashboard. The platform supports GPT-4o, Claude, and open-source models, giving you flexibility in conversation quality and cost management.

Key strengths: Stammer.ai's lead capture integration converts chatbot conversations into CRM entries automatically. Resellers report a 28% increase in client retention when lead capture is activated because business owners see direct ROI. The platform also includes a reseller portal where your clients can view analytics without seeing Stammer.ai branding.

According to Stammer.ai's 2025 partner report, the average reseller manages 34 client accounts and generates $11,200/mo in gross revenue. Their top-performing resellers focus on a single industry vertical, which we will discuss in the scaling section.

### BotPenguin

BotPenguin offers a white-label plan starting at $149/mo, making it the most affordable entry point. The platform supports website chat, WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger, Telegram, and Instagram DM bots. With over 60 pre-built templates, BotPenguin allows rapid deployment for common use cases like appointment booking, FAQ handling, and lead qualification.

BotPenguin serves over 150,000 businesses globally (company data, 2025). Their white-label tier includes custom domain hosting, removable branding, and API access for custom integrations. The tradeoff is less sophisticated AI compared to Stammer.ai. BotPenguin's bots work best for structured, rule-based conversations with an AI fallback, rather than fully open-ended AI dialogue.

For resellers targeting price-sensitive markets like local service businesses, BotPenguin's lower cost base enables competitive pricing while maintaining 55-65% margins.

### ChatLab

ChatLab positions itself as the premium option for resellers targeting mid-market clients. Plans start at $299/mo for the agency tier with white-labeling. The platform's standout feature is its knowledge base ingestion: it can scrape an entire website, PDF library, or help center and train a chatbot on that content in under 10 minutes.

ChatLab's AI accuracy benchmarks show 91% correct answer rates on domain-specific questions after knowledge base training, compared to 74% for generic prompting approaches (ChatLab internal testing, 2025). For resellers, this means fewer client complaints about chatbot accuracy and lower support overhead.

The platform also includes conversation analytics, sentiment tracking, and escalation rules that automatically hand off to human agents when the chatbot detects frustration. 43% of consumers say they prefer chatbots for simple queries but want human access for complex issues (Salesforce State of Service, 2025). ChatLab's hybrid approach addresses both needs.

## The Pricing Model: $1,500 Setup + $240/Mo Profit

The most successful white-label chatbot resellers use a two-part pricing model that maximizes upfront revenue and builds recurring income.

**Setup Fee: $1,500-$3,000 per client**

The setup fee covers initial chatbot configuration, knowledge base training, website integration, and testing. Your actual time investment is 3-5 hours per client once you have a streamlined process. At $1,500, you are earning $300-$500/hour for your setup work.

Justify the setup fee by including a strategy call, custom conversation flow design, and integration with the client's existing tools (CRM, calendar, email). According to HubSpot's 2025 Agency Pricing Report, businesses expect to pay $1,000-$5,000 for chatbot setup. Pricing at $1,500 positions you as affordable yet professional.

**Monthly Retainer: $297-$497 per client**

Your platform cost per client ranges from $15-$50/mo depending on your provider and volume tier. Charging $297-$497/mo means $240-$450 in pure recurring profit per client. The monthly fee covers ongoing chatbot management, conversation monitoring, knowledge base updates, and performance optimization.

At 20 clients on a $347/mo retainer, your monthly recurring revenue is $6,940. Subtracting platform costs of approximately $600-$1,000/mo (depending on tier and provider), you net $5,940-$6,340/mo in recurring profit, plus any setup fees from new clients.

**Usage-Based Upsells**

Add revenue with usage tiers. Charge extra for chatbots that exceed 1,000 conversations/month or need advanced features like multilingual support, voice integration, or CRM sync. According to Drift's 2025 benchmark report, the average business chatbot handles 1,847 conversations per month. Tiered pricing at 500, 2,000, and 5,000 conversations gives clients room to grow while increasing your revenue per account.

## Target Clients: Who Buys White-Label Chatbots

Not every business needs a chatbot. Focus on industries where chatbots deliver measurable ROI within the first 30 days.

**Dental and Medical Practices**

Healthcare practices lose an average of 35% of inbound calls due to hold times and after-hours inquiries (PatientPop, 2025). A chatbot that handles appointment booking, insurance questions, and post-visit follow-ups can recover 20-30% of those lost leads. The average dental patient is worth $653/year in revenue (ADA, 2025). Recovering even 5 patients per month justifies the chatbot cost 10x over.

**Real Estate Agencies**

Real estate agents receive 73% of their leads outside business hours (Zillow, 2025). A chatbot that qualifies leads, answers property questions, and schedules showings 24/7 is a competitive advantage. The average real estate commission is $8,000+. If the chatbot generates even one additional closed deal per quarter, the ROI is undeniable.

**E-Commerce Stores**

Online stores with 500-10,000 monthly visitors can increase conversion rates by 15-25% with a well-trained product recommendation chatbot (Tidio, 2025). Focus on stores in the $500K-$5M revenue range. They have enough traffic to benefit but not enough resources to build in-house.

**Law Firms**

78% of legal consumers contact multiple firms before choosing one (Clio Legal Trends Report, 2025). The first firm to respond wins the client 58% of the time. A chatbot that responds instantly, qualifies the case, and books a consultation gives law firms a measurable edge.

**Restaurants and Hospitality**

Chatbots handle reservation management, menu inquiries, and event booking. Restaurants lose an estimated $1,000/week in missed reservations from unanswered calls and messages (OpenTable, 2025). A $297/mo chatbot that recovers even 20% of that lost revenue sells itself.

## Scaling to $10K+ Per Month

Reaching $10,000/mo in net profit requires 25-35 active clients. Here is the scaling playbook.

**Phase 1: First 5 Clients (Months 1-2)**

Start with warm outreach. Contact local businesses you already patronize. Offer the first 3 clients a discounted $997 setup fee in exchange for testimonials and case studies. Install chatbots on your own demo sites to show prospects real-time examples. According to BrightLocal's 2025 survey, 84% of B2B buyers trust case studies as a primary decision factor.

**Phase 2: Systematize (Months 3-4)**

Build a repeatable onboarding process. Create a questionnaire that captures everything needed for chatbot setup: business info, common customer questions, booking URLs, and brand voice guidelines. A standardized process reduces setup time from 5 hours to 2 hours per client. Develop 3-5 industry-specific templates that require minimal customization.

**Phase 3: Outbound Sales Engine (Months 5-8)**

Hire a part-time appointment setter or use an AI outreach tool. Target 50 businesses per week in your chosen vertical. A cold email sequence with a personalized Loom video showing the prospect's website with a chatbot overlay converts at 3-5%, according to Lemlist's 2025 outreach benchmarks. That is 6-10 booked calls per month, closing 2-4 new clients.

**Phase 4: Vertical Domination (Months 9-12)**

The highest-earning resellers own a vertical. If you serve 15 dental practices, you understand their exact needs, objections, and ROI metrics. You can build pre-trained chatbot templates for that industry, reducing setup time to under 1 hour. Vertical specialization increases close rates by 40% and reduces churn by 25% (SaaS Capital, 2025) because your solution feels custom-built for their industry.

**Phase 5: Team and Partnerships (Month 12+)**

At 30+ clients, hire a chatbot manager to handle ongoing maintenance ($3,000-$4,000/mo). Partner with web design agencies, marketing consultants, and business coaches who serve your target market. Offer 15-20% referral commissions. According to PartnerStack's 2025 data, B2B SaaS companies that implement partner programs grow revenue 28% faster than those relying solely on direct sales.

## Keys to Long-Term Success

**Deliver measurable ROI.** Send clients monthly reports showing conversations handled, leads captured, and appointments booked. Clients who see data stay 3.2x longer than those who do not (ChurnZero, 2025).

**Update chatbots proactively.** Do not wait for clients to request changes. Monitor conversations weekly, identify gaps in the chatbot's knowledge, and update before the client notices. Proactive management is the primary differentiator between resellers with 5% monthly churn and those with 15%.

**Build for stickiness.** Integrate the chatbot with the client's CRM, calendar, and email systems. Every integration makes switching costly. The average SaaS product with 3+ integrations has 40% lower churn than standalone products (Profitwell, 2025).

The white-label AI chatbot business model combines low startup costs, recurring revenue, and a massive market need. The barrier to entry is effort, not capital. Resellers who commit to a vertical, systematize their sales process, and obsess over client results will build businesses worth $100K-$200K+ in annual profit within 12-18 months.
