# AI Venture Debt: How Startups Raise $5M-$50M Without Giving Up Equity

CoreWeave raised $7.5 billion in debt financing in 2025 to fund GPU infrastructure. Lambda Labs secured a $500 million credit facility to expand its AI cloud. Even smaller AI startups are accessing $5-50 million in venture debt to extend runway, finance GPU purchases, or bridge between equity rounds — all without giving up a single percentage point of ownership.

Venture debt has become the secret financing weapon for AI founders who understand that equity is the most expensive capital you will ever raise.

## What Is Venture Debt and Why AI Startups Use It

Venture debt is a loan product designed specifically for venture-backed startups. Unlike traditional bank loans, venture debt does not require profitability, hard assets, or years of financial history. Instead, lenders underwrite based on the quality of your VC backers, your growth trajectory, and your ability to raise the next equity round.

For AI startups, venture debt solves three specific problems:

**GPU financing.** A single NVIDIA H100 cluster costs $2-5 million. Financing this through equity means giving up 1-3% ownership for hardware that depreciates. Debt is cheaper for asset-heavy purchases.

**Runway extension.** Raising venture debt 3-6 months after an equity round adds 6-12 months of runway. A startup that raised $20 million Series A can add $6-8 million in venture debt, extending from 18 months to 24-30 months of runway without additional dilution.

**Bridge financing.** Between equity rounds, venture debt keeps the company operating while hitting the milestones needed for the next raise. This is particularly valuable when the AI funding market tightens.

## Venture Debt Terms for AI Startups

Typical terms vary by stage, lender, and company quality. Here is what AI startups should expect:

| Term | Typical Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Loan Size | 25-35% of last equity round | $20M Series A = $5-7M debt |
| Interest Rate | 10-14% annually | Prime + 4-7% spread |
| Term Length | 36-48 months | 3-4 year repayment |
| Interest-Only Period | 6-12 months | No principal payments initially |
| Warrant Coverage | 0.1-0.5% of company | Lender gets small equity kicker |
| Covenants | Minimum cash balance, ARR targets | Varies by lender |
| Prepayment Penalty | 1-3% in year 1, declining | Some lenders waive after 12 months |

The total cost of venture debt — interest plus warrants — typically equals 15-20% of the loan amount over its life. Compare this to the cost of equity: giving up 15-25% of your company in a Series A. For a company eventually worth $500 million, that equity is worth $75-125 million. The $5 million debt facility costs $750K-$1M all-in.

## Who Provides Venture Debt to AI Companies

### Tier 1: Dedicated Venture Debt Funds

**Western Technology Investment (WTI)**
One of the oldest venture debt providers, WTI has financed over $4 billion in venture loans. They focus on Series A through pre-IPO companies and have specific appetite for AI infrastructure. Typical facility: $5-30 million.

**Trinity Capital**
Publicly traded (TRIN), Trinity focuses on growth-stage venture lending. They have deployed over $1.5 billion and actively target AI companies with $5M+ ARR. Known for flexible structures including equipment financing for GPU clusters. Typical facility: $10-50 million.

**Horizon Technology Finance**
Another public BDC (HRZN) with $800+ million deployed to venture-backed companies. They specialize in technology and life sciences, with growing AI exposure. Typical facility: $5-25 million.

### Tier 2: Bank Venture Lending

**Silicon Valley Bank (now First Citizens)**
Despite the 2023 crisis, SVB's venture lending division remains the largest bank-based provider. They offer revolving credit lines and term loans, typically requiring the company to bank with them. AI startups with $2M+ ARR and top-tier VCs can access $3-15 million facilities. Interest rates tend to be lower (Prime + 2-4%) but with stricter covenants.

**JPMorgan Innovation Economy**
JPMorgan's startup banking division has aggressively expanded into venture lending, targeting late-seed through growth-stage companies. They compete on rate (often 1-2% below specialty lenders) but require comprehensive banking relationships. Facilities range from $5-100 million.

**HSBC Innovation Banking**
The former SVB UK operation, now owned by HSBC, provides venture debt across the US, UK, and Europe. They have been particularly active with AI companies in the $10-100M ARR range, with facilities from $5-50 million.

### Tier 3: Revenue-Based and Alternative Lenders

**Lighter Capital**
Revenue-based financing for earlier-stage AI startups. No equity dilution, no warrants. They lend 25-45% of ARR with monthly payments based on revenue. Ideal for AI companies with $100K-$3M ARR that are too early for traditional venture debt. Typical facility: $200K-$4 million.

**Pipe**
Converts recurring revenue streams into upfront capital. AI SaaS companies can trade future subscription revenue for immediate cash at a 5-10% discount. Fast funding (days, not months) for smaller amounts of $100K-$5 million.

**Capchase**
Similar to Pipe, focusing on non-dilutive financing based on recurring revenue. They have expanded into longer-term growth capital for AI companies, offering up to 12 months of revenue in upfront capital.

## When Venture Debt Makes Financial Sense

### The Math of Dilution Avoidance

Consider a real scenario: Your AI startup raised a $15 million Series A at a $60 million pre-money valuation. You own 70% post-round. You need an additional $5 million to hit Series B milestones.

**Option A: Bridge equity round at $75M pre-money**
You raise $5M, giving up 6.25% of the company. If the company reaches $500M at exit, that 6.25% is worth $31.25 million — the true cost of that $5 million.

**Option B: $5M venture debt at 12% interest, 0.25% warrant coverage**
Total interest over 3 years: approximately $900K. Warrant value at exit: $1.25 million (0.25% of $500M). Total cost: $2.15 million.

**Savings: $29.1 million in preserved equity.**

This is why every AI founder raising $10 million or more should have venture debt as part of their capital strategy.

### Ideal Timing for Venture Debt

| Timing | Strategy | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| 2-4 months after equity round | Extend runway by 6-12 months | Lenders love fresh equity backing |
| 6 months before next raise | Bridge to hit key milestones | Better metrics = better valuation |
| GPU purchase needed | Equipment financing | Asset-backed, lower rates |
| Revenue acceleration phase | Growth capital | Revenue covers debt service |

### When to Avoid Venture Debt

Venture debt is not free money. Avoid it when:

- **Burn rate exceeds revenue by 5x+.** You cannot service debt payments and lenders will require aggressive covenants.
- **No clear path to next equity round.** If you default, lenders can force liquidation or conversion at punitive terms.
- **Company is pre-revenue with no VC backing.** Traditional venture debt requires institutional VC investors as a signal of quality.
- **Your runway is already below 6 months.** Desperate borrowers get the worst terms.

## AI-Specific Venture Debt Structures

### GPU Equipment Financing

The most popular structure for AI companies. Lenders finance GPU clusters as collateral-backed loans with better terms than general venture debt:

- **Loan-to-value:** 70-80% of hardware cost
- **Interest rate:** 8-12% (lower due to collateral)
- **Term:** 24-36 months (aligned with GPU depreciation)
- **Collateral:** The GPUs themselves

CoreWeave's $7.5 billion debt facility was structured primarily as equipment financing against its massive GPU fleet. Smaller AI companies use the same structure at scale — a $3 million H100 cluster financed at 75% LTV means $2.25 million in non-dilutive capital with the GPUs as collateral.

### Revenue-Based Financing for AI SaaS

For AI companies with predictable recurring revenue, revenue-based financing offers the most founder-friendly terms:

- No warrants (zero equity dilution)
- Payments flex with revenue (lower revenue = lower payments)
- No personal guarantees
- No board seats or governance rights

The tradeoff is cost: revenue-based financing typically carries 15-25% total cost of capital, higher than traditional venture debt. But for founders who value control and flexibility, the premium is worthwhile.

### Convertible Debt with AI-Specific Terms

Some AI startups negotiate convertible notes with venture debt features:

- 12-18 month maturity
- 6-8% interest rate
- Conversion at 15-25% discount to next round
- Conversion cap at 1.5-2x current valuation

This hybrid works well for AI companies between major rounds that want the optionality of debt with potential conversion.

## Negotiating Your Venture Debt Term Sheet

### Key Negotiation Points

**Warrant coverage.** Push for 0.1-0.25% instead of the standard 0.3-0.5%. If you have multiple lenders competing, use leverage to reduce warrant coverage. Every 0.1% matters at scale.

**Interest-only period.** Negotiate for 12 months minimum. This preserves cash during the critical growth phase when you need every dollar for customer acquisition.

**Financial covenants.** Reject minimum revenue covenants tied to specific dates. Instead, accept minimum cash balance covenants (typically 3-6 months of burn in the bank). This gives you flexibility on growth timing.

**Prepayment terms.** Negotiate for no prepayment penalty after month 12. Many lenders start at 3% declining to 1% over 3 years. Strong companies can negotiate to 1% flat or no penalty after the first year.

**MAC clause.** The Material Adverse Change clause allows lenders to call the loan if something bad happens. Push for specific, objective triggers rather than subjective language. "Revenue declining 30% quarter-over-quarter" is negotiable. "Lender determines material change" is not.

French AI startup Mistral combined equity rounds with substantial debt facilities from European banks. ### Applied Intuition — Growth Debt for Sales Expansion

Applied Intuition, the autonomous vehicle simulation company, used venture debt from Silicon Valley Bank to finance its enterprise sales expansion before raising at a $6 billion valuation. ## Building Your Venture Debt Strategy

### Step 1: Start Conversations Early
Approach venture debt lenders 2-3 months after closing your equity round. Your bank balance is high, your story is fresh, and you have maximum leverage.

### Step 2: Create Lender Competition
Approach 3-5 lenders simultaneously. Share your term sheets (with permission) to drive competition on rates and warrant coverage. The difference between 0.2% and 0.5% warrant coverage is real money at exit.

### Step 3: Match Debt Structure to Use Case
GPU purchase? Equipment financing. Runway extension? Term loan. Revenue acceleration? Revenue-based financing. The right structure saves hundreds of thousands in total cost.

### Step 4: Model the Downside
Before signing, model what happens if you cannot raise your next round. Can you service the debt from revenue? What are the covenant triggers? What are the lender's remedies? Understand your worst case before committing.

Venture debt is one of the most powerful and underused tools in the AI startup financing toolkit. For founders who understand the math, it preserves millions in equity while providing the capital needed to build category-defining AI companies.
