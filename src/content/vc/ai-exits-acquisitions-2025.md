# AI Exits 2025: The Biggest Acquisitions and What They Mean for Investors

2025 saw record AI M&A activity. Google acquired Wiz for $32 billion — the largest cybersecurity acquisition in history. Wix bought Base44 for $80 million — a solo developer's 6-month project. Total AI M&A exceeded $96 billion in 2025. For investors and founders, these exits reveal clear patterns about what acquirers pay for and why.

## Patterns in AI Exits

### Pattern 1: Acqui-hires Are the New M&A
Google, Microsoft, and Amazon are buying AI companies primarily for the people, not the products. Character.AI's product was largely deprecated after acquisition — Google wanted the researchers who built it.

**What this means:** If you are building an AI company and your team includes top AI researchers, your company has a floor valuation based on talent alone. AI researchers command $500K-1M+ annual compensation. A team of 10 is worth $5M-10M+ in acqui-hire value regardless of product success.

### Pattern 2: Infrastructure > Applications
The highest-value exits are in AI infrastructure (Wiz for security, Scale AI for data) rather than AI applications. Infrastructure companies provide picks and shovels that every AI builder needs.

**What this means:** If you are building to sell, infrastructure plays command higher multiples (15-40x revenue) than application plays (5-15x revenue).

### Pattern 3: Speed of Exit Is Accelerating
Base44 went from idea to $80M exit in 6 months. This was unthinkable in previous tech cycles. AI dramatically reduces the time from concept to valuable product.

**What this means:** The window for building and selling an AI company is compressed. Founders who ship fast and find product-market fit quickly can reach exit-worthy valuations in months, not years.

### Pattern 4: Big Tech Is the Primary Buyer
Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, and Apple are responsible for the majority of AI acquisitions by value. They have the balance sheets ($500B+ combined cash) and the strategic motivation (AI is existential for their businesses).

**What this means:** Build what big tech needs but does not want to build internally. Specialized AI capabilities, niche domain expertise, and unique data assets are what they buy.

## For Angel Investors and VCs

**Exit timeline:** AI startup exits happen in 2-5 years on average, significantly faster than traditional tech startups (5-10 years). This means faster returns on invested capital.

**Exit multiples:** AI companies are selling at 20-50x revenue (infrastructure) and 10-20x revenue (applications). These multiples are elevated by the AI gold rush but may normalize.

**The acquirer landscape:** Five companies (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Apple, Meta) account for 70%+ of AI acquisition value. Understanding their strategic priorities is essential for predicting which startups they will buy.

## The Bottom Line

$96 billion in AI M&A in 2025 — a record. The patterns are clear: big tech buys infrastructure, talent, and speed. Founders who build defensible AI capabilities with fast time-to-value create the most attractive acquisition targets. For investors, AI provides faster exits at higher multiples than any previous tech cycle. The exit window is wide open.
