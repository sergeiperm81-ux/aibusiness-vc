## Routes to Public Markets: Which Path Suits AI Companies

### Traditional IPO

**How it works:** Company files S-1 with the SEC, conducts a roadshow, and investment banks price and sell shares to institutional investors. The company raises new capital.

**Best for:** AI companies that need capital. Anthropic needs billions for compute. CoreWeave needs equity to balance its $7.5B+ in debt. Traditional IPOs raise $500M-$5B in fresh capital while creating a public currency for acquisitions and employee compensation.

**Cost:** 5-7% underwriting fees plus $2-5M in legal and accounting costs. A $2B IPO costs roughly $110-145M in fees.

**Recent AI example:** Reddit's March 2024 IPO raised $748 million at a $6.4 billion valuation. Despite mixed early trading, it demonstrated public market appetite for AI-adjacent platforms. By early 2026, Reddit's market cap had exceeded $30 billion, rewarding IPO investors with 4x+ returns.

### Direct Listing

**How it works:** Company lists existing shares on a public exchange without raising new capital. No underwriters set the price — the market discovers it through supply and demand on day one.

**Best for:** Profitable AI companies that do not need capital but want liquidity for employees and early investors. Stripe and Canva are ideal direct listing candidates — both are reportedly profitable or near-profitable with no urgent need for outside capital.

**Cost:** Significantly cheaper. No underwriting fees (saving 5-7%). Advisory fees of $10-30M versus $100M+ for a traditional IPO.

**Historical examples:** Spotify (2018), Slack (2019), Coinbase (2021), and Roblox (2021) all used direct listings. The results were mixed — Spotify and Coinbase succeeded, while Slack eventually sold to Salesforce at a lower valuation than its direct listing peak.

### SPAC Mergers

**How it works:** A Special Purpose Acquisition Company raises capital through its own IPO, then merges with a private company to take it public. The private company becomes public through the merger rather than its own IPO.

**2025-2026 reality:** The SPAC market has cooled dramatically from its 2021 peak. In 2021, 613 SPACs raised $162 billion. In 2025, fewer than 50 SPACs raised under $10 billion. However, AI-focused SPACs are seeing renewed interest.

**Best for:** AI companies in the $1-5 billion valuation range that want to go public faster than the 12-18 month traditional IPO timeline. SPACs can close in 3-6 months and allow the target company to share forward projections that are not permitted in traditional IPOs.

| SPAC Characteristic | 2021 Peak | Current 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| Average SPAC size | $300M | $150-200M |
| Time to merge | 3-6 months | 4-8 months |
| Redemption rate | 20-40% | 60-80% (investors more cautious) |
| Founder promote | 20% | 10-15% (reduced for better deals) |
| Target valuation range | $1-20B | $500M-$5B |

**AI-relevant SPAC activity:** Several AI-focused SPACs formed in late 2025 and early 2026, including vehicles from investors with deep AI expertise. While the volume is far below 2021, quality has improved. These SPACs target specific AI verticals — defense AI, healthcare AI, and AI infrastructure — where domain expertise adds value beyond capital.

## Financial Benchmarks for AI IPOs

Public markets apply different standards than private markets. Here is what AI companies need to demonstrate:

### Revenue Requirements

No hard minimum, but practical thresholds exist:

| Revenue Benchmark | Market Reception |
|---|---|
| Below $200M ARR | Difficult unless exceptional growth (100%+ YoY) |
| $200M-$500M ARR | Viable with 50%+ growth and clear path to profitability |
| $500M-$1B ARR | Strong candidate, public market ready |
| Above $1B ARR | Premium reception, can price aggressively |

### The Rule of 40 for AI Companies

Public market investors use the Rule of 40: revenue growth rate plus profit margin should exceed 40%. For AI companies, the bar is higher — investors expect Rule of 60 or better for AI-premium pricing.

| Scenario | Growth | Margin | Rule Score | Market Response |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hypergrowth AI | 100% | -40% | 60 | Premium pricing |
| Growth AI | 60% | -10% | 50 | Fair pricing |
| Mature AI | 30% | +15% | 45 | Standard pricing |
| Struggling AI | 20% | -20% | 0 | Discounted or delayed |

Databricks reportedly hits 60%+ revenue growth with improving margins, placing it firmly in premium IPO territory. CoreWeave's revenue is growing over 100% but margins are compressed by infrastructure costs — its Rule of 40 score depends heavily on how the market treats gross margins versus operating margins.

## How to Invest in Pre-IPO AI Companies

### Secondary Market Platforms

For accredited investors, secondary markets provide access to pre-IPO AI shares:

**Forge Global (FRGE)**
The largest secondary marketplace for pre-IPO shares. Forge has facilitated $15+ billion in transactions across 500+ companies. AI companies including Anthropic, Databricks, and Stripe shares trade regularly on Forge. Minimum investment typically $100,000.

**EquityZen**
Focuses on individual accredited investors with lower minimums ($10,000-$50,000). EquityZen structures investments through SPVs that purchase shares from employees and early investors. They have facilitated investments in OpenAI, Canva, and Stripe pre-IPO shares.

**Hiive**
A newer platform gaining market share with a bid-ask marketplace model similar to a stock exchange. Hiive offers real-time pricing data and typically lower fees (3-5% versus Forge's 5-7%). They have seen strong demand for AI company shares.

**CartaX (Carta)**
Carta's secondary marketplace connects shareholders directly with buyers, leveraging Carta's cap table data for 40,000+ companies. CartaX handles compliance and settlement, making transactions smoother for both sides.

### Pricing Dynamics in Secondary Markets

Pre-IPO AI shares typically trade at a 10-30% discount to the company's last primary round valuation. This discount reflects:

- **Illiquidity premium:** You cannot sell easily, so you need a discount.
- **Information asymmetry:** Secondary buyers have less information than primary round investors.
- **Transfer restrictions:** Some companies limit or prohibit secondary sales, constraining supply.

However, for hot AI companies, secondary shares sometimes trade at a premium to the last round. OpenAI shares reportedly traded at 15-25% above the $300 billion valuation on secondary markets in early 2026, driven by retail and institutional demand.

| Company | Last Round Valuation | Secondary Market Premium/Discount | Typical Lot Size |

| Stripe | $91B (internal) | -10-15% discount | $100K+ |

### Risks of Pre-IPO Investing

**Lockup periods.** After IPO, insiders and secondary market buyers typically face a 90-180 day lockup. The stock can drop significantly when lockup expires and selling pressure hits.

**Valuation compression.** Private market valuations do not always hold in public markets. WeWork's infamous example aside, even strong companies can see 20-40% valuation compression from last private round to IPO price.

**Limited exit options.** If the company delays or cancels its IPO, secondary shares become even more illiquid. Some investors in late-stage companies have waited 5-7 years for liquidity events.

## 2026 AI IPO Market Predictions

**CoreWeave will set the tone.** As the first major AI infrastructure company to go public, CoreWeave's IPO performance will signal public market appetite for AI. A strong debut opens the floodgates. A weak debut delays the pipeline.

**Databricks likely files by mid-2026.** With $2.4B+ ARR and strong growth, Databricks is the most obvious traditional IPO candidate. CEO Ali Ghodsi has publicly discussed IPO readiness. Expect a $60-80 billion valuation target.

**At least 3-5 AI companies will reach public markets in 2026.** Beyond CoreWeave and likely Databricks, expect 1-3 additional AI companies to go public through traditional IPOs, direct listings, or SPAC mergers.

**The AI premium will hold for leaders but compress for followers.** The first wave of AI IPOs will benefit from novelty and scarcity premiums. Subsequent offerings will face more scrutiny as public market investors develop better frameworks for AI company valuation.

For investors, the 2026 AI IPO wave represents a generational wealth-creation opportunity. The key is distinguishing companies with durable AI advantages from those riding temporary hype — and positioning capital accordingly before the IPO window opens.
